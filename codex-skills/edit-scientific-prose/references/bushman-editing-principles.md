# Bushman-Derived Editing Principles

Source: Frederic D. Bushman, *Improving Your Scientific Writing*, v67, University of Pennsylvania Bushman Lab.

Use these principles as editing heuristics, not rigid rules.

## Sentence-Level Edits

- Prefer short declarative sentences.
- Put the subject and verb early.
- Remove long opening modifier clauses when they force rereading.
- Delete weak intensifiers and editorializing.
- Remove words that add no information.
- Replace vague abstractions with specific experimental terms.
- Use active voice when it clarifies agency.
- Keep verb tense consistent within a section.

## Paragraph-Level Edits

- Start paragraphs with strong topic sentences.
- Use one main idea per paragraph.
- Make each paragraph earn its place in the argument.
- Preserve transitions that help the reader follow logic; delete transitions that merely pad.

## Scientific Tone

- Let facts carry the story.
- Do not tell readers something is interesting, novel, or exciting. Show the result and let readers infer importance.
- Keep qualifications that protect accuracy, but remove hedging that only reflects uncertainty in the prose.
- Prefer precision over flourish.

## Editing Pass

1. Find the claim.
2. Cut clutter.
3. Simplify sentence structure.
4. Strengthen topic sentences.
5. Check tense and technical meaning.
6. Read the revised paragraph for flow.
