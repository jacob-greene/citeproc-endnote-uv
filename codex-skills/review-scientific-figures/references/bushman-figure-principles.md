# Bushman-Derived Figure Principles

Source: Frederic D. Bushman, *Improving Your Scientific Writing*, v67, University of Pennsylvania Bushman Lab.

## Figure Purpose

- A figure should make the main result visible.
- Remove clutter to make room for information.
- Organize panels around the scientific argument.
- Keep comparisons adjacent and visually aligned.

## Visual Design

- Label directly when possible.
- Use readable text and consistent scale.
- Use color to encode information, not decoration.
- Keep axes, units, ordering, and symbols consistent.
- Avoid unnecessary 3D effects, shadows, and chart junk.

## Figure Legends

- State what the figure shows.
- Define symbols, colors, abbreviations, units, and statistics.
- Include sample size, replicate type, and test where needed.
- Do not use the legend to compensate for an unclear visual design.

## Image Integrity

- Apply contrast or intensity adjustments uniformly to the whole image.
- Disclose image manipulations.
- Do not add, remove, selectively enhance, or hide data features.
- Make boundaries clear when panels come from separate experiments.
- Follow journal image guidelines.
