# Bushman-Derived Grant Principles

Source: Frederic D. Bushman, *Improving Your Scientific Writing*, v67, University of Pennsylvania Bushman Lab.

## Reviewer Model

Reviewers are busy, tired, and reading many applications. Write so a reviewer can quickly understand the problem, become interested, and advocate for the proposal.

## Proposal Style

- Use simple short sentences.
- Use short paragraphs with one idea each.
- Make the hypothesis, objective, aims, and payoff easy to find.
- Follow the instructions exactly.
- Explain how the proposal matches the funder or mechanism.
- Explain unfamiliar technology clearly enough for a non-specialist reviewer.

## Specific Aims

Each aim should include:

- An action verb.
- A testable question or deliverable.
- A concise rationale.
- An expected outcome.
- A fallback or interpretive path when risk is substantial.

## Feasibility and Risk

- Use preliminary data to show capability and de-risk the project.
- Identify real risks and alternatives.
- Avoid pretending every experiment is guaranteed to work.
- Make clear what will be learned even if a result differs from expectation.

## Advocate Test

After drafting, ask whether a panel member could summarize the proposal in two sentences and defend why it should be funded.
