# Bushman-Derived Manuscript Principles

Source: Frederic D. Bushman, *Improving Your Scientific Writing*, v67, University of Pennsylvania Bushman Lab.

## Core Style

- Write simple prose.
- Use short paragraphs with one idea each.
- Begin paragraphs with topic sentences.
- Cut every unnecessary word.
- Let the facts carry the story.

## Manuscript Logic

- Build the paper around the scientific question, not the chronology of labor.
- Keep background selective and relevant to the current claim.
- In results prose, tell readers why each experiment was done before describing what happened.
- Pair conclusions with the data and controls that justify them.
- Address alternative explanations where doing so increases credibility.
- Use the discussion to explain what changed in understanding, not to repeat all results.

## Response Letters

- Keep responses short, complete, and respectful.
- Make it easy for editors and reviewers to see what changed.
- Lead with meaningful added data or analysis when it exists.
- Quote or paraphrase each concern, then answer directly.

## Common Failure Modes

- Literature-list introductions.
- Results sections that report techniques without explaining the question.
- Discussions that restate the paper without synthesis.
- Overclaiming from limited data.
- Long paragraphs that mix background, result, and interpretation.
