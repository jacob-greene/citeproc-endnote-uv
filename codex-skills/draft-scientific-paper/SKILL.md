# Draft Scientific Paper

Use this skill to build manuscript prose around a clear scientific story.

## Workflow

1. Determine the section type: abstract, introduction, results, discussion, methods-adjacent prose, cover letter, response letter, or thesis chapter.
2. State the main claim or question in plain language before drafting.
3. Organize paragraphs as claim -> evidence -> implication.
4. Use topic sentences to keep the reader oriented.
5. Avoid exhaustive literature listing. Integrate prior work only when it explains the current question, gap, or interpretation.
6. Explain experiments in the order that makes the logic easiest to follow, not necessarily the order they were performed.
7. Include controls and alternative explanations where they strengthen credibility.
8. Distinguish observed results from interpretation.
9. Keep limitations honest and specific.
10. End sections by pointing forward to the next logical question or conclusion.

## Section Patterns

**Introduction**: Move from broad problem to specific gap to the work's central question. Keep background selective.

**Results**: Open each subsection with the point of the experiment. Then describe design, result, control, and conclusion.

**Discussion**: Start with what was learned. Then connect to prior work, implications, limits, and future tests.

**Response to reviewers**: Keep responses clipped, complete, and respectful. Lead with added data or concrete changes when present.
